What Problem Does It Solve?
Perpetual futures exchanges charge a funding rate -- a periodic payment between long and short traders to keep contract prices aligned with spot. When one exchange charges a high rate and another charges a low rate (or pays traders), a delta-neutral spread exists. This system captures that spread by going long on the exchange with the lower rate and short on the one with the higher rate, resulting in a market-neutral profit.
